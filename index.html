Yellow — Risk moved from dissolved Cat 4 to another category (V2)

V1 → V2: The original 25-risk baseline is consolidated — Cat 4 (Educational & Epistemic Integrity) is dissolved and its three risks redistributed: misinformation moves to Cat 5 as risk 5.1, while the two cognitive/academic risks are absorbed into a restructured Cat 8, now renamed Cognitive Mismatch & Desirable Difficulties. One genuinely new risk is added (8.4 — Autonomy Displacement), yielding 24 active risks across 7 active categories.
V2 → V3: The taxonomy is extended beyond KORA's original benchmark scope to capture a broader range of child-AI harms: 7 new risks are introduced across existing categories (Privacy Violations in Cat 7; Sensorimotor & Social Displacement in Cat 8) and through a new Cat 9 — Systemic & Societal Risks — which names collective harms (Cultural Erosion, AI Divide, Epistemic Power Concentration, Cognitive Homogenization…) that affect children at the societal level, not just individually. V3 is intentionally wider in scope than what KORA currently benchmarks.
